Healthy Tips

To help children develop healthy eating habits:

- Provide plenty of vegetables, fruits, and whole-grain products.

- Include low-fat or non-fat milk or dairy products, including cheese and yogurt.

- Choose lean meats, poultry, fish, lentils, and beans for protein.

- Encourage your family to drink lots of water.

- Limit sugary drinks.

- Limit consumption of sugar and saturated fat.

Healthy tips to abdominal pain in children

- Include more fibrous foods in your childâ€™s diet to keep constipation and indigestion at bay.

- Refrain from feeding your child too close to his bedtime, as it may cause indigestion.

- Do not let your child overeat at one meal. Serve smaller, frequent meals.

- Encourage your child to wash his hands well before and after every meal, to keep him safe from bacterial and viral infections.

- Monitor the fluid intake of your child, as lack of fluids can cause constipation.

Healthy tips for Cough

- Wash your hands often

- Avoid others who are sick

- Prevent cross-contamination

- Clean affected surfaces

- Encourage your child to drink plenty of fluids

- Give a spoonful of honey (over age 1)

- Encourage your child to get plenty of rest and sleep.



Healthy Tips to Diarrhea

- Eat more foods containing starch. Starchy foods are easily digested during diarrhea.

- Drink extra water. Avoid all fruit juices and carbonated drinks.

- Milk and milk products are fine.

- Avoid beans or any other foods that cause loose stools.

Healthy tips to ear problem

- Vaccinate your child. Make sure your child is up to date on vaccinations.

- Wash your hands.

- Breastfeed.

- Avoid bottles in bed.

- Avoid smoke exposure.

- Decrease pacifier use.

- Switch your child to a smaller daycare center.



Healthy Tips to eye problem

- Rest the eyes. Taking regular breaks after every 30-40 minutes of reading, writing, or playing the computer is recommended.

- Protection against sun damage.

- Increase time spent outdoors.

- Intake of fruits, vegetables and fatty fish.

- Schedule regular eye check ups.

- Encourage healthy eye care habits.

Healthy tips to fever

- A Sponge Bath or Warm Bath.

- Use the Fan Judiciously.

- Feed your Child at Regular Intervals.

- Keep your Child Hydrated.

- Choose Light Clothing.

- Stay Indoors.

- Rest and Sleep are Healers.



Healthy Tips to headache

- Maintain regular sleep habits

- Exercise regularly because children should get at least 30-40minutes of physical activity three to four days per week

- Eat a well-balanced diet. Avoid meal skipping.

- Stay well hydrated

- Limit caffeine intake

Healthy tips to nasal congestion

- Drink a lot of fluids

- Get a plenty of rest

- Sleeping upright

- Keeping the air clean



Healthy Tips to vomiting

- Sit down or lie propped up.

- Avoid physical activity.

- Drink something sugary like ginger ale or Gatorade.

- Avoid alcohol, caffeine, and acidic drinks like orange juice.

- Suck ice chips or have a cold drink.

- Avoid oily and spicy foods.

- Practice deep breathing exercises.

Healthy tips to skin rashes

- Keep kids cool.

- Keep skin bare.

- Don't scratch.

- Avoid dirty pools.

- Don't allow young children in spas or hot tubs.

- Talk with your pediatrician.



Healthy Tips to sore throat

- Not sharing food utensils and glasses with others

- Avoiding contact with people who have sore throats or cold symptoms

- Sip warm liquids.

- Eat cold or frozen liquids (such as ice pops)

- Gargle with saltwater.

Healthy tips to urinary problem

- Give Her Lots of Water. Make your child drink as much water as possible.

- Make Her Drink Fruit Juices.

- Feed Probiotics.

- Give Lemon Juice.

- Avoid Acidic Food and Drinks.

- Warm Bath.

- Wipe From Front to Back (for girls).

- Tell to Urinate Often.
